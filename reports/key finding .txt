11) Key findings text for report

Use these formats in your report after looking at your actual outputs.

Product findings

Write like this:

Finding 1: Profitability is concentrated in a limited set of products

A relatively small proportion of products contributes the majority of total profit, indicating dependency on a narrow set of strong-performing items.

Finding 2: High sales does not always lead to high profit quality

Several products generate strong revenue but deliver below-average gross margin, suggesting pricing inefficiency or elevated cost burden.

Finding 3: Some high-margin products are under-scaled

A group of products demonstrates strong margin performance but low sales contribution, making them suitable candidates for promotion and wider market focus.

Division findings
Finding 4: Division-level imbalance exists

Comparison of revenue contribution and profit contribution shows that some divisions generate significant revenue but underperform on profit, indicating structural margin weakness.

Finding 5: Financial efficiency varies across divisions

Divisions with higher gross margin contribute disproportionately more profit relative to their revenue share, showing stronger portfolio efficiency.

Cost findings
Finding 6: Cost-heavy products reduce portfolio quality

Products with high cost-to-sales ratios and weak margin should be reviewed for sourcing optimization, supplier renegotiation, or pricing revision.

Pareto findings
Finding 7: Profit concentration risk is present

A limited share of products drives 80% of total profit, which may expose the business to concentration risk if demand patterns shift.

Factory findings
Finding 8: Factory performance is uneven

Factory-level aggregation highlights differences in sales, profit, and margin efficiency, suggesting that operational contribution is not uniformly distributed across production sites.
